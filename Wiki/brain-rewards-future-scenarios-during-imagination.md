# Brain Rewards Future Scenarios During Imagination

> Stub created from [[Sources/bm2]]. Expand manually.

This Hebrew-language article from Haaretz reviews research on 'the simulation effect' — how the brain's reward mechanisms are activated during future-oriented imagination, not just during actual experiences. The mechanism serves an adaptive function by reinforcing planning and solution evaluation, but can also anchor maladaptive worry patterns around negative scenarios. The research suggests that imagined future scenarios carry inherent neurobiological reward value, making mental simulation a powerful but double-edged cognitive tool.
