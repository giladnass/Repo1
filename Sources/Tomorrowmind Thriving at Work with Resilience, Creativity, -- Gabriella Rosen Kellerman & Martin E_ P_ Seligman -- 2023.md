# Tomorrowmind Framework for Workplace Resilience and Thriving

## Summary

"Tomorrowmind" by Gabriella Kellerman and Martin E.P. Seligman examines how workers can thrive amid rapid technological change through the lens of psychological science and labor history. The book introduces five critical psychological skills (PRISM): Resilience/cognitive agility, Meaning/mattering, Social support (rapid rapport), Prospection, and Creativity/innovation—designed to help individuals navigate workplace disruption. Through the story of Graeme Payne's career transformation after being scapegoated for the Equifax data breach, the authors illustrate how these skills enable professional reinvention and recovery. The work is grounded in positive psychology research and positions current workplace challenges as the latest in a series of human labor transformations, each extracting psychological costs that modern science can help address.

## Key Findings

- The human brain evolved over 95% of our history for foraging work (hunting, gathering, fishing)—creating a fundamental mismatch between our native psychology and modern work demands that explains widespread workplace distress
- Each major labor transformation (agricultural, industrial, technological) has extracted significant psychological costs: agriculture introduced anxiety about distant threats; industrialization created unprecedented monotony and alienation from labor; today's technological transformation threatens job displacement (800 million workers by 2030) and rising burnout (affecting roughly half the US workforce)
- The authors identify five psychological powers essential for 21st century workplace thriving (PRISM): Resilience/cognitive agility, Meaning/mattering, Social support via rapid rapport, Prospection, and Creativity/innovation
- Modern work challenges include: average job tenure of only 2.8 years for workers aged 25-34, projected 25-30% remote workforce, doubling of loneliness prevalence in twenty years, and 120,000 excess deaths annually in the US attributable to workplace stress
- The research draws on partnerships between the authors' BetterUp Labs and major corporations, combining positive psychology with behavioral science innovation to investigate workplace flourishing at scale

## Questions Raised

- How can individuals systematically develop each of the PRISM skills, and what specific evidence-based interventions are most effective?
- What structural and organizational changes are needed to support employee thriving beyond individual skill development?
- How do the psychological costs of remote work and continued technological acceleration compare to previous labor transformations, and can interventions be scaled effectively?
- To what extent can the PRISM framework address systemic inequalities in workplace access, security, and support?
