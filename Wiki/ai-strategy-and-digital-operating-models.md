# AI Strategy and Digital Operating Models

> Stub created from [[Sources/Competing in the Age of AI _ Strategy and Leadership When -- Marco Iansiti; Karim R_ Lakhani -- 2020]]. Expand manually.

This chapter introduces the transformative impact of artificial intelligence and digital networks on business, arguing that AI is becoming the universal "runtime" or operational foundation for companies. The authors explain how digital operating models enable three critical advantages—scale (infinite replicability at near-zero marginal cost), scope (easy connectivity across activities), and learning (continuous improvement through data)—that traditional operating models cannot match. Through detailed case studies of Amazon, Walmart, and Tencent/WeChat, the chapter illustrates how AI-driven firms are fundamentally different: they improve with growth rather than suffering from complexity costs, creating new competitive dynamics beyond traditional disruption theory. The authors position this transformation as affecting every industry and sector, not just tech companies, establishing a framework for understanding how strategy, leadership, and organizational structure must evolve in the age of AI.
