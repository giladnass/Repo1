# Non-Medication ADHD Treatments for Children and Teens

## Summary

This workbook provides 162 non-medication strategies for managing ADHD in children and teens, covering diagnosis, mindfulness, psychotherapy, neurofeedback, nutrition, sleep, social skills, and organizational techniques. It emphasizes holistic, evidence-based tools for clinicians and parents, offering practical activities and resources to address ADHD symptoms comprehensively. The book aims to reduce reliance on medication by integrating psychotherapy, complementary therapies, and lifestyle adjustments, supported by expert endorsements and research.

## Key Findings

- The book offers a comprehensive, non-medication approach to ADHD management, including 162 tools across 11 sections like neurofeedback, mindfulness, and nutrition.
- It emphasizes evidence-based strategies for improving executive function, emotional regulation, and social skills, tailored for clinicians and parents.
- The workbook addresses ADHD comorbidities (e.g., anxiety, sleep disorders) and environmental factors (e.g., toxins, screen time) that exacerbate symptoms.
- Experts highlight its practicality, accessibility, and value as a 'one-stop' resource for holistic ADHD treatment.
- The book includes diagnostic tools (e.g., QEEG, TOVA) and progress-tracking methods to ensure personalized, effective interventions.
