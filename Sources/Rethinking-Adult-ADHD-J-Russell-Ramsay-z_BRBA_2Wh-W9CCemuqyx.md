# Cognitive Therapy for Adult ADHD

## Summary

This book, 'Rethinking Adult ADHD: Helping Clients Turn Intentions into Actions,' explores cognitive behavior therapy (CBT) as a framework for understanding and treating adult ADHD. It emphasizes the role of cognitive themes, particularly self-regulatory efficacy, in addressing implementation deficits—challenges in executing plans and tasks. The text bridges neurobiological foundations (e.g., dopamine deficiency, executive function impairments) with clinical strategies to help clients manage procrastination, emotion regulation, and organizational difficulties. Key contributions include a unified CBT model for ADHD, case examples, and practical interventions to convert intentions into actionable steps.

## Key Findings

- Adult ADHD is conceptualized as a neurodevelopmental disorder of impaired self-regulation, with executive function deficits (e.g., behavioral inhibition, working memory) central to its presentation.
- Cognitive interventions in CBT target maladaptive thoughts and beliefs, particularly procrastination and escape-avoidance behaviors, to improve task initiation and follow-through.
- Self-regulatory efficacy—a specific form of self-efficacy—is proposed as the core cognitive theme in adult ADHD, mediating the gap between intentions and actions.
- The book integrates neurobiological insights (e.g., dopamine deficiency, default mode network dysregulation) with psychological models to explain ADHD's impact on daily functioning.
- Procrastination and emotional dysregulation are linked to maladaptive cognitive distortions and require tailored CBT strategies to address underlying thought patterns.

## Questions Raised

- How can cognitive interventions be optimized for clients with comorbid anxiety or depression to avoid over-reliance on CBT alone?
- What long-term outcomes are associated with the proposed CBT model for adult ADHD compared to traditional behavioral strategies?
- How do cultural or socioeconomic factors influence the effectiveness of cognitive interventions in ADHD treatment?
- What role does technology (e.g., digital reminders, apps) play in enhancing the implementation of CBT-derived coping strategies for ADHD?
