# Cognitive Therapy for Adult ADHD

> Stub created from [[Sources/Rethinking-Adult-ADHD-J-Russell-Ramsay-z_BRBA_2Wh-W9CCemuqyx]]. Expand manually.

This book, 'Rethinking Adult ADHD: Helping Clients Turn Intentions into Actions,' explores cognitive behavior therapy (CBT) as a framework for understanding and treating adult ADHD. It emphasizes the role of cognitive themes, particularly self-regulatory efficacy, in addressing implementation deficits—challenges in executing plans and tasks. The text bridges neurobiological foundations (e.g., dopamine deficiency, executive function impairments) with clinical strategies to help clients manage procrastination, emotion regulation, and organizational difficulties. Key contributions include a unified CBT model for ADHD, case examples, and practical interventions to convert intentions into actionable steps.
