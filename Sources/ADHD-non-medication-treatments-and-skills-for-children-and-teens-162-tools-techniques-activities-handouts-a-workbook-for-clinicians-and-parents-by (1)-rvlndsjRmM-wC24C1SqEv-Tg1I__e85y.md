# Non-Medication ADHD Treatments for Children

## Summary

This comprehensive workbook provides 162 non-medication tools, techniques, and strategies for clinicians and parents to help children and teens with ADHD. It covers diagnosis, neurofeedback, mindfulness, psychotherapy, nutrition, sleep, social skills, organizational strategies, and parenting techniques. The book emphasizes holistic, evidence-based approaches to managing ADHD symptoms and improving quality of life, supported by endorsements from leading ADHD experts.

## Key Findings

- The book offers a structured, evidence-based framework for diagnosing ADHD using tools like DSM-5 criteria, QEEG, and continuous performance tests (TOVA/IVA).
- Non-medication interventions such as neurofeedback, mindfulness, and frontal lobe training show efficacy in improving self-regulation and reducing ADHD symptoms.
- Nutritional strategies, including addressing food sensitivities and optimizing fatty acid/mineral intake, are highlighted as critical for brain health in ADHD.
- Parenting and school-based strategies (e.g., organizational systems, sleep hygiene, and social skills training) are provided to create supportive environments.
- The workbook includes reproducible handouts and worksheets for tracking progress, making it a practical resource for clinicians and families.

## Questions Raised

- How effective are non-medication approaches compared to traditional pharmacological treatments for ADHD in long-term outcomes?
- What barriers might limit access to specialized interventions like neurofeedback or QEEG for families with limited resources?
- How can the integration of multiple non-medication strategies be tailored to individual differences in ADHD subtypes or comorbid conditions?
- What further research is needed to validate the efficacy of complementary therapies (e.g., sensory integration, movement techniques) in ADHD management?
